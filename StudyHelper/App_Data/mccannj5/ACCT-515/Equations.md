Equation Name: The Accounting Equation
Equation Summary: The fundamental identity underlying the balance sheet, showing that a company's resources equal the claims against those resources
Equation: Assets = Liabilities + Stockholders Equity

Equation Name: Net Income
Equation Summary: Overall performance measure on the income statement combining operating and peripheral activity
Equation: Net Income = Revenues - Expenses + Gains - Losses

Equation Name: Gross Profit
Equation Summary: Measures profitability after covering the direct cost of the goods sold, before operating expenses
Equation: Gross Profit = Net Sales - Cost of Goods Sold

Equation Name: Operating Income
Equation Summary: Reflects the operating performance of the entity after both cost of goods sold and operating expenses
Equation: Operating Income = Net Sales - Cost of Goods Sold - Operating Expenses

Equation Name: Book Value of Equity
Equation Summary: The accounting value of ownership claims, equal to total assets minus total liabilities
Equation: Book Value = Total Assets - Total Liabilities

Equation Name: Market Capitalization
Equation Summary: The market value of a company's equity based on its current share price
Equation: Market Capitalization = Market Value per Share x Shares of Stock Outstanding

Equation Name: Retained Earnings Rollforward
Equation Summary: Tracks how the retained earnings balance changes from one period to the next based on income and dividends
Equation: Ending Retained Earnings = Beginning Retained Earnings + Net Income - Dividends Declared

Equation Name: Working Capital
Equation Summary: Measures a company's ability to repay short-term debt in absolute dollar terms
Equation: Working Capital = Current Assets - Current Liabilities

Equation Name: Current Ratio
Equation Summary: Measures short-term liquidity in relative terms by comparing current assets to current liabilities
Equation: Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities

Equation Name: Quick (Acid-Test) Ratio
Equation Summary: A stricter liquidity test that includes only the most liquid current assets
Equation: Quick Ratio = (Cash + Short-Term Investments + Receivables) / Current Liabilities

Equation Name: Debt Ratio
Equation Summary: The percentage of a company's assets financed through borrowing
Equation: Debt Ratio = Total Liabilities / Total Assets

Equation Name: Debt-to-Equity Ratio
Equation Summary: The amount of borrowed capital for every dollar of equity investment
Equation: Debt-to-Equity Ratio = Total Liabilities / Total Equity

Equation Name: Return on Investment (Return on Assets)
Equation Summary: Shows the company's performance relative to its resource base
Equation: Return on Investment = Net Income / Average Total Assets

Equation Name: ROI Decomposition
Equation Summary: Breaks return on investment into a profitability component and a productivity component
Equation: Return on Investment = (Net Income / Net Sales) x (Net Sales / Average Assets)

Equation Name: Return on Equity
Equation Summary: The amount of net income earned as a percentage of stockholders equity, indicating return generated for owners
Equation: Return on Equity = Net Income / Average Stockholders Equity

Equation Name: Reconciling Net Income to Operating Cash Flow
Equation Summary: Adjusts accrual-basis net income for noncash items and changes in operating current assets and liabilities to derive operating cash flow
Equation: Operating Cash Flow = Net Income + Depreciation Expense - Increase (+ Decrease) in Operating Current Assets + Increase (- Decrease) in Operating Current Liabilities

Equation Name: Net Realizable Value of Accounts Receivable
Equation Summary: The amount of receivables expected to be collected in cash after removing estimated uncollectible amounts
Equation: Net Realizable Value = Accounts Receivable - Allowance for Doubtful Accounts

Equation Name: Bad Debts Expense - Percent of Receivables Method
Equation Summary: Balance sheet approach that adjusts the allowance account to a desired ending balance based on a percentage of receivables
Equation: Bad Debts Expense = Desired Ending Allowance Balance - Existing Allowance Balance Before Adjustment

Equation Name: Bad Debts Expense - Percent of Credit Sales Method
Equation Summary: Income statement approach that estimates bad debts expense directly as a percentage of credit sales for the period
Equation: Bad Debts Expense = Credit Sales x Estimated Bad Debt Percentage

Equation Name: Simple Interest
Equation Summary: Calculates interest earned or owed on a note based on principal, annual rate, and the fraction of a year outstanding
Equation: Interest = Principal x Rate x Time

Equation Name: Total Cash Collected (or Paid) at Note Maturity
Equation Summary: Total cash exchanged when a note is settled, combining the principal with all accrued interest
Equation: Total Cash at Maturity = Principal + (Principal x Rate x Time)

Equation Name: Accounts Receivable Turnover
Equation Summary: Reveals how many times receivables are collected during the period, indicating how quickly the company converts receivables to cash
Equation: Accounts Receivable Turnover = Net Credit Sales (or Total Revenue) / Average Accounts Receivable

Equation Name: Average Days to Collect Receivables
Equation Summary: Converts the receivables turnover rate into an average number of days needed to collect receivables
Equation: Average Days to Collect Receivables = 365 Days / Accounts Receivable Turnover

Equation Name: Cost of Goods Sold
Equation Summary: The cost of inventory sold during the period, computed from beginning inventory, purchases, and ending inventory
Equation: Cost of Goods Sold = Beginning Inventory + Purchases - Ending Inventory

Equation Name: Cost of Goods Available for Sale
Equation Summary: The total cost of inventory that could have been sold during the period, before splitting between sold and unsold units
Equation: Cost of Goods Available for Sale = Beginning Inventory + Purchases

Equation Name: Weighted Average Cost per Unit
Equation Summary: Spreads the total cost of goods available for sale evenly across all units available, used under the weighted average inventory method
Equation: Weighted Average Cost per Unit = Cost of Goods Available for Sale / Units Available for Sale

Equation Name: Inventory Turnover
Equation Summary: Measures how many times a company sells and replaces its inventory during a period
Equation: Inventory Turnover = Cost of Goods Sold / Average Inventory

Equation Name: Average Days to Sell Inventory
Equation Summary: Converts inventory turnover into the average number of days it takes to sell inventory
Equation: Average Days to Sell Inventory = 365 Days / Inventory Turnover

Equation Name: Total Acquisition Cost of PP\&E
Equation Summary: All costs necessary to bring a long-term asset to the condition and location needed for its intended use
Equation: Total Acquisition Cost = Purchase Price - Discounts + Sales Tax + Freight + Installation Costs + Other Costs to Prepare for Use

Equation Name: Basket Purchase Allocation
Equation Summary: Allocates a lump-sum purchase price among multiple assets acquired together, based on their relative appraised (fair) values
Equation: Allocated Cost of Asset = (Fair Value of Asset / Total Fair Value of All Assets) x Total Purchase Price

Equation Name: Depreciable Cost
Equation Summary: The portion of an asset's cost that will be allocated to expense over its useful life
Equation: Depreciable Cost = Acquisition Cost - Estimated Residual (Salvage) Value

Equation Name: Straight-Line Depreciation
Equation Summary: Allocates an equal amount of depreciation expense to each period of an asset's useful life
Equation: Annual Depreciation Expense = (Cost - Residual Value) / Useful Life

Equation Name: Double-Declining-Balance Rate
Equation Summary: The fixed percentage rate applied each year under the double-declining-balance method, equal to twice the straight-line rate
Equation: DDB Rate = 2 x (1 / Useful Life)

Equation Name: Double-Declining-Balance Depreciation Expense
Equation Summary: An accelerated depreciation method that applies a fixed rate to the asset's remaining book value each year
Equation: Depreciation Expense = Beginning Book Value x DDB Rate

Equation Name: Units-of-Production Depreciation Rate
Equation Summary: Calculates the depreciation cost assigned to each unit of output or use over an asset's estimated total capacity
Equation: Depreciation Rate per Unit = (Cost - Residual Value) / Estimated Total Units of Output

Equation Name: Units-of-Production Depreciation Expense
Equation Summary: Bases depreciation expense on the actual level of asset use during the period
Equation: Depreciation Expense = Depreciation Rate per Unit x Units of Output for the Period

Equation Name: Book Value of a Depreciable Asset
Equation Summary: The remaining undepreciated cost of a long-term asset carried on the balance sheet
Equation: Book Value = Acquisition Cost - Accumulated Depreciation

Equation Name: Gain or Loss on Disposal of PP\&E
Equation Summary: Determines whether disposing of a long-term asset results in a gain or a loss by comparing proceeds to book value
Equation: Gain (Loss) on Disposal = Proceeds Received - Book Value at Date of Disposal

Equation Name: PPE Turnover
Equation Summary: Measures how many dollars of sales a company generates for each dollar invested in property, plant, and equipment
Equation: PPE Turnover = Net Sales / Average Net Property Plant and Equipment

Equation Name: Sales Tax Payable
Equation Summary: The liability a retailer owes to the taxing authority based on taxable sales collected from customers
Equation: Sales Tax Payable = Sales Amount x Sales Tax Rate

Equation Name: Estimated Warranty Liability
Equation Summary: Estimates the warranty obligation recognized in the period of sale based on expected failure rates and repair costs
Equation: Estimated Warranty Liability = Units Sold x Estimated Failure Rate x Average Repair Cost per Unit

Equation Name: Periodic Interest Rate for Installment Notes
Equation Summary: Converts an annual interest rate into the rate applicable to each compounding (payment) period
Equation: Interest Rate per Period = Annual Interest Rate / Number of Compounding Periods per Year

Equation Name: Installment Loan Interest Expense per Period
Equation Summary: Computes the interest portion of an installment loan payment based on the outstanding balance at the start of the period
Equation: Interest Expense = Beginning Note Balance x Interest Rate per Period

Equation Name: Installment Loan Principal Reduction per Period
Equation Summary: Determines how much of a fixed installment payment reduces the loan principal after covering the interest
Equation: Principal Reduction = Payment Amount - Interest Expense

Equation Name: Proceeds from Stock Issuance
Equation Summary: The total cash a company receives when issuing new shares of stock to investors
Equation: Total Proceeds from Stock Issuance = Number of Shares Issued x Issue Price per Share

Equation Name: Common (or Preferred) Stock Account Balance
Equation Summary: The par value component of stockholders equity recorded when shares are issued
Equation: Common Stock Account = Number of Shares Issued x Par Value per Share

Equation Name: Additional Paid-in Capital
Equation Summary: The amount received from investors in excess of a stock's par value upon issuance
Equation: Additional Paid-in Capital = Total Proceeds from Issuance - Par Value of Shares Issued

Equation Name: Total Cash Dividend
Equation Summary: The total dollar amount of a cash dividend distribution to common or preferred shareholders
Equation: Total Cash Dividend = Dividend per Share x Number of Shares Outstanding

Equation Name: Treasury Stock Cost
Equation Summary: The total cost recorded when a company repurchases its own shares of stock
Equation: Treasury Stock Cost = Number of Shares Repurchased x Repurchase Price per Share

Equation Name: Stock Dividend Transfer from Retained Earnings
Equation Summary: The amount capitalized out of retained earnings into contributed capital accounts when a stock dividend is declared
Equation: Amount Transferred = Shares Distributed x Market Value per Share

Equation Name: Average Cost per Unit (Manufacturing)
Equation Summary: Determines the cost to manufacture each unit of product by spreading total production cost over all units produced
Equation: Average Cost per Unit = Total Cost / Number of Units

Equation Name: Total Product Cost
Equation Summary: Sums the three components of manufacturing cost that are capitalized as inventory until the product is sold
Equation: Total Product Cost = Direct Materials + Direct Labor + Manufacturing Overhead

Equation Name: Cost of Goods Manufactured
Equation Summary: Standard GAAP formulation showing how work in process inventory converts into completed goods during a period
Equation: Cost of Goods Manufactured = Beginning Work in Process Inventory + Total Manufacturing Costs - Ending Work in Process Inventory

Equation Name: Cost of Goods Sold (Manufacturer)
Equation Summary: Standard GAAP formulation showing how finished goods inventory flows into cost of goods sold for a manufacturing company
Equation: Cost of Goods Sold = Beginning Finished Goods Inventory + Cost of Goods Manufactured - Ending Finished Goods Inventory

Equation Name: Contribution Margin
Equation Summary: The amount of sales revenue remaining after variable expenses that is available to cover fixed expenses and generate profit
Equation: Contribution Margin = Sales Revenue - Variable Expenses

Equation Name: Contribution Margin Ratio
Equation Summary: Expresses contribution margin as a percentage of sales, showing how much of each sales dollar remains after variable costs
Equation: Contribution Margin Ratio = Contribution Margin / Sales Revenue

Equation Name: CVP Net Income Equation
Equation Summary: The core cost-volume-profit relationship linking sales, variable expenses, fixed expenses, and profit
Equation: Sales Revenue - Variable Expenses - Fixed Expenses = Profit

Equation Name: Break-Even Point in Units
Equation Summary: Calculates the number of units that must be sold for total contribution margin to equal fixed expenses
Equation: Break-Even Point in Units = Fixed Expenses / Unit Contribution Margin

Equation Name: Break-Even Point in Sales Dollars
Equation Summary: Calculates the sales revenue needed to cover fixed expenses using the contribution margin ratio
Equation: Break-Even Point in Sales Dollars = Fixed Expenses / Contribution Margin Ratio

Equation Name: Units Sold to Earn Target Profit
Equation Summary: Extends the break-even formula to solve for the sales volume needed to achieve a specified target profit
Equation: Units Sold to Earn Target Profit = (Fixed Expenses + Target Profit) / Unit Contribution Margin

Equation Name: Margin of Safety (Dollars)
Equation Summary: Measures how far actual or budgeted sales can fall before the company reaches its break-even point
Equation: Margin of Safety = Budgeted (Actual) Sales - Break-Even Sales

Equation Name: Margin of Safety (Percentage)
Equation Summary: Expresses the margin of safety as a percentage of budgeted or actual sales
Equation: Margin of Safety Percentage = Margin of Safety / Budgeted (Actual) Sales

Equation Name: Magnitude of Operating Leverage
Equation Summary: Measures how sensitive net income is to a percentage change in sales, driven by the proportion of fixed costs in the cost structure
Equation: Magnitude of Operating Leverage = Contribution Margin / Net Income

Equation Name: Percentage Change in Net Income (Operating Leverage)
Equation Summary: Uses the operating leverage factor to translate a percentage change in sales into the resulting percentage change in net income
Equation: Percentage Change in Net Income = Percentage Change in Sales x Magnitude of Operating Leverage

Equation Name: Total Variable Cost Behavior
Equation Summary: Describes how total variable costs change in direct proportion to activity level, starting from zero
Equation: Y = b X

Equation Name: Total Fixed Cost Behavior
Equation Summary: Describes how total fixed costs remain constant regardless of activity level within the relevant range
Equation: Y = a

Equation Name: Total Mixed Cost Behavior
Equation Summary: Describes a cost that contains both a fixed component and a variable component that increases with activity
Equation: Y = a + b X

Equation Name: Total Cost Equation
Equation Summary: General formula combining fixed and variable cost components to estimate total cost at a given activity level
Equation: Total Cost = Total Fixed Costs + (Variable Cost per Unit x Number of Units)

Equation Name: High-Low Method - Variable Cost per Unit
Equation Summary: Estimates the variable cost per unit of activity using the highest and lowest activity levels observed in a data set
Equation: Variable Cost per Unit = (Cost at Highest Activity Level - Cost at Lowest Activity Level) / (Highest Activity Level - Lowest Activity Level)

Equation Name: High-Low Method - Total Fixed Cost
Equation Summary: Backs out total fixed cost by subtracting estimated variable cost from total cost at either the high or low activity point
Equation: Total Fixed Cost = Total Cost at Activity Point - (Variable Cost per Unit x Activity Level at that Point)

Equation Name: Coefficient of Determination (R-squared)
Equation Summary: A statistical measure from least-squares regression showing the percentage of variation in cost explained by the activity variable
Equation: R-squared = Percentage of Variation in Dependent Variable Explained by Independent Variable
