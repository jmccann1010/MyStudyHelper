Equation Name: The Accounting Equation
Equation Summary: The fundamental equation of accounting that underlies the balance sheet, showing that a company's resources (assets) equal the claims against those resources from creditors and owners.
Equation: Assets = Liabilities + Stockholders' Equity

Equation Name: Expanded Accounting Equation
Equation Summary: Expands stockholders' equity into its contributed and earned components, useful for tracing transaction effects through the balance sheet.
Equation: Assets = Liabilities + Contributed Capital + Retained Earnings

Equation Name: Net Income
Equation Summary: The overall measure of company performance reported on the income statement, capturing both operating and peripheral activities.
Equation: Net Income = Revenues - Expenses + Gains - Losses

Equation Name: Gross Profit
Equation Summary: Measures profitability from core product/service sales before considering operating overhead.
Equation: Gross Profit = Net Sales - Cost of Goods Sold

Equation Name: Operating Income
Equation Summary: Reflects the performance of a company from its core, ongoing business operations, excluding financing and investing items.
Equation: Operating Income = Net Sales - Cost of Goods Sold - Operating Expenses

Equation Name: Retained Earnings (Ending Balance)
Equation Summary: The accumulated net income kept in the business over its economic life less amounts paid out as dividends, updated each period.
Equation: Ending Retained Earnings = Beginning Retained Earnings + Net Income - Dividends

Equation Name: Book Value of Equity
Equation Summary: The accounting (carrying) value of the owners' interest in a company as reported on the balance sheet.
Equation: Book Value = Total Assets - Total Liabilities

Equation Name: Market Capitalization (Market Value)
Equation Summary: The total market value of a company's outstanding common stock; compared to book value to assess investor expectations.
Equation: Market Capitalization = Market Price per Share x Shares Outstanding

Equation Name: Net Realizable Value of Accounts Receivable
Equation Summary: The amount of receivables a company expects to actually collect in cash, after estimating uncollectible accounts.
Equation: Net Realizable Value = Accounts Receivable - Allowance for Doubtful Accounts

Equation Name: Bad Debts Expense (Percent of Receivables Method - Balance Sheet Approach)
Equation Summary: Used in the allowance method to adjust the Allowance for Doubtful Accounts to a desired ending balance based on receivables; the plug to that account is bad debts expense.
Equation: Bad Debts Expense = Desired Ending Allowance Balance - Existing Allowance Balance Before Adjustment

Equation Name: Desired Allowance Balance (Percent of Receivables)
Equation Summary: Estimate of uncollectible accounts based on the year-end receivables balance multiplied by an estimated default rate.
Equation: Desired Allowance Balance = Ending Accounts Receivable x Estimated Uncollectible Percentage

Equation Name: Bad Debts Expense (Percent of Sales Method - Income Statement Approach)
Equation Summary: Estimates bad debts expense directly as a percentage of credit sales for the period; emphasizes matching expenses to the revenue that generated them.
Equation: Bad Debts Expense = Credit Sales x Estimated Uncollectible Percentage

Equation Name: Working Capital
Equation Summary: An absolute (dollar) measure of short-term liquidity indicating the cushion of current assets above current obligations.
Equation: Working Capital = Current Assets - Current Liabilities

Equation Name: Current Ratio
Equation Summary: A relative measure of short-term liquidity; historically a benchmark of 2:1 was considered healthy, with concerns below that level.
Equation: Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities

Equation Name: Quick (Acid-Test) Ratio
Equation Summary: A more rigorous liquidity measure that excludes inventory and prepaid expenses, focusing only on the most liquid current assets.
Equation: Quick Ratio = (Cash + Short-Term Investments + Receivables) / Current Liabilities

Equation Name: Debt Ratio
Equation Summary: Solvency measure indicating the percentage of a company's assets financed by creditors; higher values indicate greater financial leverage and risk.
Equation: Debt Ratio = Total Liabilities / Total Assets

Equation Name: Debt-to-Equity Ratio
Equation Summary: Solvency measure showing the dollars of debt financing per dollar of equity financing; reflects capital structure risk.
Equation: Debt-to-Equity Ratio = Total Liabilities / Total Equity

Equation Name: Return on Investment (Return on Assets)
Equation Summary: A profitability measure showing how effectively a company uses its asset base to generate earnings for all capital providers.
Equation: Return on Investment = Net Income / Average Total Assets

Equation Name: Return on Investment (DuPont Decomposition)
Equation Summary: Decomposes ROI into a profitability component (return on sales) and a productivity component (asset turnover), revealing the drivers of returns.
Equation: Return on Investment = (Net Income / Net Sales) x (Net Sales / Average Total Assets)

Equation Name: Profit Margin (Return on Sales)
Equation Summary: The profitability component of ROI; the percentage of each sales dollar that becomes net income.
Equation: Profit Margin = Net Income / Net Sales

Equation Name: Asset Turnover
Equation Summary: The productivity component of ROI; how many dollars of sales the company generates per dollar of assets.
Equation: Asset Turnover = Net Sales / Average Total Assets

Equation Name: Return on Equity
Equation Summary: Measures the company's ability to generate a return for its common stockholders relative to their equity investment.
Equation: Return on Equity = Net Income / Average Stockholders' Equity

Equation Name: Earnings per Share (Basic)
Equation Summary: The amount of net income associated with each share of common stock; a widely watched per-share profitability measure.
Equation: Basic EPS = (Net Income - Preferred Dividends) / Weighted Average Common Shares Outstanding

Equation Name: Net Change in Cash (Statement of Cash Flows)
Equation Summary: The total change in cash for the period is the sum of cash flows from the three classified business activities.
Equation: Net Change in Cash = Cash Flow from Operating Activities + Cash Flow from Investing Activities + Cash Flow from Financing Activities

Equation Name: Ending Cash Balance
Equation Summary: Reconciles the beginning and ending cash positions reported across the balance sheet and statement of cash flows.
Equation: Ending Cash = Beginning Cash + Net Change in Cash

Equation Name: Operating Cash Flow (Indirect Method)
Equation Summary: Starts with accrual net income and reconciles to cash from operations by adjusting for non-cash items and changes in operating-related working capital accounts.
Equation: Operating Cash Flow = Net Income + Non-Cash Expenses - Increases in Current Assets + Decreases in Current Assets + Increases in Current Liabilities - Decreases in Current Liabilities

Equation Name: Cash Collected from Customers
Equation Summary: Converts accrual-basis sales revenue to cash basis by adjusting for the change in accounts receivable during the period.
Equation: Cash Collected from Customers = Sales Revenue + Beginning Accounts Receivable - Ending Accounts Receivable

Equation Name: Cash Paid for Operating Expenses (Wages Example)
Equation Summary: Converts accrual-basis expense to cash basis by adjusting for the change in the related accrued liability during the period.
Equation: Cash Paid for Wages = Wage Expense + Beginning Wages Payable - Ending Wages Payable

Equation Name: Bank Reconciliation - Bank Side
Equation Summary: Adjusts the bank statement balance for items the bank has not yet processed to arrive at the true (correct) cash balance.
Equation: Adjusted Bank Balance = Balance per Bank Statement + Deposits in Transit - Outstanding Checks + or - Bank Errors

Equation Name: Bank Reconciliation - Book Side
Equation Summary: Adjusts the company's general ledger cash balance for items not yet recorded by the company to arrive at the true (correct) cash balance.
Equation: Adjusted Book Balance = Balance per General Ledger + Collections by Bank - Bank Service Charges - NSF Checks + or - Book Errors

Equation Name: Operating Cycle (Concept)
Equation Summary: The average time it takes a business to convert cash into inventory, inventory into receivables, and receivables back into cash; defines the boundary between current and noncurrent items.
Equation: Operating Cycle = Days in Inventory + Days in Accounts Receivable
